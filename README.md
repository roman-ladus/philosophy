# 1. CORE IDEAS (BIG PICTURE)

## What is philosophy doing across the course?

* Challenging assumptions
* Expanding understanding
* Revealing **limits**:

  * Knowledge (Kant, Nagel)
  * Language (Wittgenstein)
  * Certainty (Russell)
  * Freedom (Sartre vs Oshana)

---

## The Core Tensions (VERY IMPORTANT)

### 1. Freedom vs Constraint

* **Sartre**: total freedom → total responsibility
* **Oshana**: real autonomy requires social conditions

---

### 2. Subjective vs Objective

* **Nagel**: experience = subjective (“what-it-is-like”)
* Science = objective → cannot fully explain mind

---

### 3. Sayable vs Unsayable

* **Wittgenstein**:

  * Facts = sayable
  * Ethics = beyond language

---

### 4. Progress vs Limits

* **Transhumanism**: improve humanity
* Critique: risk to identity, equality, meaning

---

### 5. Certainty vs Doubt

* **Russell**: philosophy valuable because it creates uncertainty

---

### 6. Autonomy vs Authority

* **Kant**: think for yourself (enlightenment)
* Requires freedom of reasoning

---

# 2. CORE ARGUMENTS (MINIMAL MAPS)

## KANT (Enlightenment)

* Immaturity = reliance on others
* Cause = fear + laziness
* Solution = **use your own reason**
  → Requires **freedom of thought**

---

## RUSSELL (Value of Philosophy)

* Philosophy does not give answers
* But:

  * Expands thinking
  * Reduces dogmatism
    → Value = **intellectual transformation**

---

## NAGEL (Consciousness)

* Experience = subjective (point of view)
* Science = objective
  → Cannot fully explain consciousness

---

## WITTGENSTEIN (Ethics & Language)

* Language describes facts
* Ethics ≠ facts
  → Ethics cannot be fully expressed

(Later:)

* Meaning = use (language-games)
  → Ethics works in practice

---

## TRANSHUMANISM

* Enhance humans → reduce suffering
* Problem:

  * Inequality
  * Identity loss
  * Free will concerns

---

## SARTRE (Existentialism)

* No fixed human nature

* You choose who you are
  → Total responsibility

* Bad faith:

  * Denying freedom

---

## OSHANA (Autonomy)

* Internal freedom not enough
* Need:

  * Real options
  * Social conditions
    → Autonomy is **socially embedded**

## Confusions to Avoid

* Freedom ≠ autonomy
* Useful ≠ valuable (Russell)
* Unsayable ≠ meaningless (Wittgenstein)
* Subjective ≠ unreal (Nagel)
* Natural ≠ good (Transhumanism debate)

# 6. CONNECTIONS (EXAM GOLD)

## Shared Theme: LIMITS

* Kant → limits of guidance
* Nagel → limits of knowledge
* Wittgenstein → limits of language
* Russell → limits of certainty
* Oshana → limits of freedom

---

## Biggest Contrast

* **Sartre**: you are always free
* **Oshana**: sometimes you are not

→ This is one of the strongest essay pairings
